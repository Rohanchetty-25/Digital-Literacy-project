# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students

# General Cyber Safety Tips

1. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** – Activate 2FA on all your important accounts, including email, social media, and banking apps. This adds an extra layer of security even if your password is compromised.

2. **Create Strong, Unique Passwords** – Use a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Never reuse passwords across multiple accounts. Consider using a password manager to keep track securely.

3. **Verify Before Clicking** – Always hover over links to see the actual URL before clicking. If an email or message creates urgency or asks for personal information, verify the sender through official channels first.

4. **Keep Software Updated** – Regularly update your phone, laptop, and apps. Security patches fix vulnerabilities that cybercriminals exploit.

5. **Think Before You Share** – Avoid posting personal information like your phone number, address, travel plans, or Aadhaar details publicly on social media.

## Financial & UPI Safety Tips

6. **Never Share OTP or UPI PIN** – Banks, payment apps, and legitimate organizations will never ask for your OTP (One-Time Password) or UPI PIN over phone, email, or text. Treat any such request as a red flag.

7. **Use Official Payment Apps Only** – Always use trusted apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or your bank's official app. Avoid clicking on payment links sent by unknown contacts or making payments through unverified websites.

8. **Check UPI Transaction Notifications** – Enable SMS and app notifications for all UPI transactions. If you receive a notification for a transaction you did not authorize, report it immediately to your bank and cyber helpline.

# Reporting Channels

- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** www.cybercrime.gov.in – File complaints online for all types of cybercrimes.
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** 1930 – 24x7 toll-free number for reporting financial fraud and seeking immediate assistance.
- **Local Police:** Visit your nearest cyber crime police station for serious cases.

---

*Remember: In case of cyber fraud, report within the first 15-20 minutes to increase chances of recovering lost money.*
